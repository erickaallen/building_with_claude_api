================================================================================
                  STREAMING SERVICE CHURN ANALYSIS REPORT
                        MAJOR DRIVERS OF CHURN
================================================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This analysis examines 500 streaming service users to identify the key factors
driving customer churn. The overall churn rate is 38.6%, with significant 
variation across different user segments and behaviors.

================================================================================
MAJOR CHURN DRIVERS (Ranked by Importance)
================================================================================

1. TOTAL VIEWING HOURS (Importance: 0.2376) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   STRONGEST PREDICTOR OF CHURN
   
   Key Findings:
   • Churned users: 66.6 hours/month average
   • Retained users: 83.2 hours/month average
   • Difference: 16.6 hours (20% less engagement)
   • Correlation with churn: -0.246 (moderate negative)
   
   Insight: Users who watch significantly less content are at much higher
   risk of churning. This is the single most important indicator of churn.
   
   Recommendation: Implement early warning system for users whose viewing
   hours decline month-over-month.


2. AVERAGE SESSION DURATION (Importance: 0.2022) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   SECOND STRONGEST PREDICTOR
   
   Key Findings:
   • Churned users: 49.4 minutes average
   • Retained users: 57.8 minutes average
   • Difference: 8.3 minutes (14% shorter sessions)
   • Correlation with churn: -0.218 (moderate negative)
   
   Insight: Shorter viewing sessions suggest users aren't finding content
   compelling enough to stay engaged. This indicates content quality or
   relevance issues.
   
   Recommendation: Analyze what causes short sessions and improve content
   recommendations to keep users engaged longer.


3. NUMBER OF UNIQUE TITLES WATCHED (Importance: 0.1724) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   STRONG CONTENT EXPLORATION INDICATOR
   
   Key Findings:
   • Churned users: 19.5 titles average
   • Retained users: 23.7 titles average
   • Difference: 4.3 titles (18% less variety)
   • Correlation with churn: -0.224 (moderate negative)
   
   Insight: Users who explore less content are more likely to churn. This
   suggests they're not finding diverse content that interests them or
   struggle with content discovery.
   
   Recommendation: Improve content discovery features and personalized
   recommendations to encourage exploration.


4. BINGE WATCHING SESSIONS (Importance: 0.1109) ⭐⭐⭐
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   ENGAGEMENT DEPTH INDICATOR
   
   Key Findings:
   • Churned users: 6.2 sessions average
   • Retained users: 7.7 sessions average
   • Difference: 1.5 sessions (20% fewer)
   • Correlation with churn: -0.237 (moderate negative)
   
   Insight: Fewer binge-watching sessions indicate lower engagement with
   series content. Users aren't getting hooked on shows.
   
   Recommendation: Promote series that encourage binge-watching and improve
   autoplay features.


5. CUSTOMER SERVICE INTERACTIONS (Importance: 0.1003) ⭐⭐⭐
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   PROBLEM INDICATOR
   
   Key Findings:
   • Churned users: 3.2 interactions average
   • Retained users: 2.5 interactions average
   • Difference: 0.7 more interactions (28% more)
   • Correlation with churn: +0.279 (POSITIVE - only positive correlation!)
   
   Insight: More customer service interactions strongly correlate with
   churn. Users contacting support are likely experiencing problems that
   lead to dissatisfaction.
   
   Recommendation: 
   - Proactively reach out to users with multiple CS contacts
   - Address root causes of common issues
   - Improve self-service options to prevent problems


6. TOP GENRE PREFERENCE (Importance: 0.0994) ⭐⭐
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   CONTENT LIBRARY QUALITY INDICATOR
   
   Churn Rates by Genre:
   • Horror: 52.3% churn (HIGHEST)
   • Thriller: 48.3% churn
   • Action: 44.6% churn
   • Romance: 41.8% churn
   • SciFi: 40.5% churn
   • Drama: 35.3% churn
   • Comedy: 33.0% churn
   • Documentary: 25.9% churn (LOWEST)
   
   Insight: Horror and Thriller fans churn at 2x the rate of Documentary
   fans. This suggests content library gaps in these genres.
   
   Recommendation: Expand Horror and Thriller catalogs with quality content.
   Study what Documentary fans appreciate about the content.


7. MONTHLY COST / SUBSCRIPTION TIER (Importance: 0.0429 / 0.0344) ⭐
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   PRICING AND VALUE PERCEPTION
   
   Churn Rates by Tier:
   • Basic ($7.99): 43.5% churn (HIGHEST)
   • Standard ($12.99): 39.5% churn
   • Premium ($17.99): 24.1% churn (LOWEST)
   
   Key Finding:
   • Basic tier has 80% higher churn than Premium
   • Price itself is less important than perceived value
   • Premium users get more value/features, driving retention
   
   Insight: The Basic tier may lack features that drive engagement, or
   attract price-sensitive customers with lower loyalty.
   
   Recommendation: 
   - Enhance Basic tier value proposition
   - Promote Premium upgrades with targeted benefits
   - Consider middle-tier improvements for Standard users

================================================================================
KEY INSIGHTS & PATTERNS
================================================================================

Pattern 1: THE ENGAGEMENT CASCADE
   Low viewing hours → Short sessions → Limited exploration → CHURN
   These metrics together paint a picture of disengaged users who aren't
   finding value in the service.

Pattern 2: THE PROBLEM ESCALATION
   Technical/content issues → CS contacts → Unresolved frustration → CHURN
   Users who need support are already at risk; poor resolution accelerates
   churn.

Pattern 3: THE TIER EFFECT
   Basic tier → Fewer features → Lower engagement → Higher churn
   Premium tier → More features → Higher engagement → Lower churn
   The tier itself matters less than the engagement it enables.

Pattern 4: THE CONTENT GAP
   Genre preferences (Horror/Thriller) with inadequate library depth lead
   to higher churn. Content quality matters more than quantity.

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

Churn in this streaming service is primarily driven by USER ENGAGEMENT LEVELS
rather than price sensitivity. The top 4 drivers are all engagement-related:
viewing hours, session duration, content exploration, and binge-watching.

The data shows a clear pattern: engaged users stay, disengaged users leave.
The key to reducing churn is identifying disengagement early and intervening
with relevant content, improved experiences, and proactive support.

Customer service interactions serve as a warning signal - these users are
already experiencing problems. Quick, effective resolution is critical.

Content library quality varies by genre, with Horror and Thriller showing
significant gaps that drive churn among those fan bases.

The subscription tier effect is really an engagement effect - Premium users
get features that drive more engagement, leading to better retention.

BOTTOM LINE: Focus on driving engagement through better content discovery,
personalized recommendations, and proactive issue resolution. Monitor the
four key engagement metrics as leading indicators of churn risk.
